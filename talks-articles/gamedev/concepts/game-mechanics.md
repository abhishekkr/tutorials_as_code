
## Game Mechanics

> sources: [Indie Game Clinic](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nkLmjJK3vOw)

* `Rules` of `If This Then That`. Rule is unit of logic. Mechanic is Rule Unit of Gameplay.

* Mechanics include Player/GameAI's Action, Resultant-Impact and Goals. Rule specific of Mechanics define how the Game behave.

* Gamefeel is derived from (& contributes to) core mechanics, fundamental to Game. Mechanics controls Gameplay, Gameplay controls Gamer Experience.
> GamePlay is using Mechanics to achieve the goal of Game. How using mechanics in a combo helps Player have the intended Gaming experience?

* Visual elements must contribute to Game Systems mechanics understanding to Player. Like traversal mechanics.
> Prototype with Fake/Limited System for the Mechanics to finalize. Then detail out the systems that benefit from and accentuate the mechanics.

* Identify the role a Mechanic plays. What makes different Mechanics work well together or not?

* Expressive Mechanics that are unimportant to Game progression are very important as well, as they make Player feel more in control and the GamePlay more fun.
> The feeling of able to Mess About (like able to break a non-consequntial item, just because Player wants to). Little things matter.
> Interpreted as Developer effort and attention to details.

* Cognitive Mechanics give a good Storyline experience. Noticing things, giving some extra visual effort for something noticeable that is in viewport of Player.
> Like able to read text on the Piece of paper. Player verbs to enable are Judge, Deduce, Decide.
